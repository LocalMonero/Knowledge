Monero Mining History

Monero has always been an ASIC-resistant coin. It was originally built with on Cryptonight, a memory hard hashing algorithm. The idea was anyone could mine on a regular CPU and have a chance at earning Monero while supporting the network, and building ASICs would be extremely expensive for a small edge; however the sheer price of Monero still drove ASIC manufacturers into the market, and changing the hashing algorithm every 6 months earned heavy community criticism. So in May 2018, a new idea to hash based on random code execution was born in November 2019, after completing 4 audits, RandomX went live.
